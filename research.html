Research

My research lies at the intersection of data science, animal welfare science, and AI bias. My previous research focused on advancing automated methods for behavior monitoring, redefining dominance hierarchy calculations, creating novel approaches for lameness detection, and understanding behavioral baselines for individual animals from sensor data. Currently, my work explores the representation bias of livestock farming in generative AI.
